In my third D3 data visualization, I used a radial graph to represent the winner's share of the purse for key golf tournaments in 2024. The graph features dots arranged on multiple concentric rings, with the size of each dot corresponding to the winner’s share amount. The outermost ring displays larger dots, representing higher winner shares, while the dots become progressively smaller as they move toward the center of the graph. This visual structure enables users to quickly identify the tournaments with the largest winner shares by simply looking at the dot sizes.

The radial graph is designed to be interactive, offering users the ability to filter the dots in and out based on the FedExCup points players can win in each tournament. This filtering feature provides a deeper layer of engagement, allowing users to explore the data through different lenses. Additionally, a draggable slider gives users the flexibility to adjust the layout of the radial graph, transforming it from a circular arrangement to a linear one. This makes it easier to compare the sizes of the winner’s share amounts side by side, offering a clearer visual comparison between tournaments.

This visualization effectively combines a unique radial layout with interactive features to offer both a functional and aesthetically pleasing experience. The dynamic nature of the graph allows users to explore the data in different ways, making it easier to understand the relationship between winner share amounts and the corresponding FedExCup points. The flexibility of the design ensures that users can engage with the data in a way that suits their preferences, providing insights without overwhelming the viewer.
