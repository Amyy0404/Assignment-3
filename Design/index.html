WIREFRAMES

HOME PAGE

On the homepage, I kept the heading nice and big to grab the user's attention right away. As they scroll down, three image buttons— 'My Designs', 'Theory', and 'Visualisations'—appear smoothly, which makes the experience feel fluid and gives users a clear choice of where to go next. I wanted these buttons to act as natural entry points to the main sections of the site.


To make the buttons more interactive, I added a hover effect where they enlarge when the mouse moves over them, then shrink back once the mouse is removed. It’s a simple but effective way to show users that these buttons are clickable. At the bottom of the page, I’ve placed the footer so that important info like contact details and navigation options are always accessible.

VISUALISATION PAGE

The visualisation page keeps a consistent design with a large, clear heading at the top, making the purpose of the page obvious right away. The navigation bar is straightforward and placed at the top, so users can easily switch between sections. On this page, users will be able to choose between two visualisations through button images. VISUALISATION 1

The visualisation 1 page starts with a large, bold heading to grab attention, and the navigation bar is kept clear and simple at the top for easy access. This page showcases my first data visualisation, focusing on the top golf players of 2024. When users change the selection, the bars adjust smoothly, shrinking and shifting to reveal the updated rankings. I've also added a tooltip feature, so when users hover over each bar, they’ll get more detailed information about that player, enhancing the interactive experience.

VISUALISATION 2

This page features my second data visualisation, which displays the 2024 tournaments, their purse sizes, and the player winnings. Instead of a bar graph, I’ve used bubbles of different sizes and colours to represent the tournament winnings, making the data visually engaging. The larger the bubble, the higher the winnings, while the colours help distinguish different tournaments. To enhance interactivity, when the user hovers over any of the bubbles, a tooltip appears with more detailed information, providing an intuitive way for users to explore the data.

THEORY PAGE

The Theory page is designed to offer users flexible access to my essays and reflections. For users who prefer to download the content, there’s a convenient download option that provides the full Word document, allowing them to read or reference it offline at their leisure. This feature supports users who may need a formal copy for research, citation, or personal archiving.

For users who want a quick, accessible read without downloading, the "Read Me" toggle button enables in-page reading directly on the website. This streamlined option is ideal for those who want to quickly review the content or read on-the-go without waiting for a download. The toggle feature makes it easy to switch between a brief overview and a deeper, in-depth reading experience, ensuring the page is versatile and user-friendly for all preferences.

SURVEY PAGE

The survey page is designed to provide users with a straightforward and user-friendly experience. The form layout is centered on the page, keeping it simple and easy to navigate. Users are asked basic questions, including their name, email, and favorite golf club, selected from a dropdown menu. The page also includes a rating slider for users to score their experience on the website, as well as a time selector for their preferred tee-off time. For additional feedback, a message box allows users to leave any further comments. The clean layout and minimal design help keep the survey accessible and focused on user feedback.

To ensure accuracy and usability, the survey page includes JavaScript validation. This script checks each field to confirm it meets the required format, such as verifying that the email includes an "@" symbol or that the phone number contains only digits. When all fields are correctly filled out, users receive a confirmation message, and the form is reset to ensure a smooth experience for any future submissions. This careful attention to form validation helps maintain data quality and enhances the overall user experience.

VISUALISATION PAGE

The visualization page in this design gives users an interactive experience, allowing them to explore different visualizations seamlessly. Users can select a specific visualization by clicking on button images that represent each option. For ease of navigation, an arrow icon cycles through the available visualizations, changing both the displayed button image and relevant content automatically. This approach keeps the interface clean while ensuring that users can move intuitively between visualizations.

Each selected visualization comes with a unique heading and description that adapts based on the current selection, ensuring that users have context and insights on what they are viewing. This dynamic setup promotes a smooth, guided exploration of information, enhancing both user engagement and comprehension as they browse through various data representations.

VISUALISATION 1 PAGE

In this data visualization, users interact with a sleek, responsive bar graph showcasing the top golf players of 2024 and their rankings. The primary display consists of simple bars representing each player’s ranking, ensuring a clean and accessible layout. As users hover over each bar, a tooltip appears to reveal more details—such as the player’s name, ranking position, and points—providing quick, relevant insights without overwhelming the visual.

A dropdown menu adds flexibility, allowing users to filter the data by top 10, 20, or 30 players. Selecting an option smoothly transitions the graph to display the selected range, adjusting both the bars and tooltip content seamlessly. This dynamic filtering gives users control over the level of detail they see, catering to both casual viewers interested in top players and more dedicated fans looking to explore rankings in depth.

VISUALISATION 2 PAGE

For the second data visualization, I’ve designed an engaging bubble chart that smoothly slides in from the left side of the screen and settles at the center, immediately drawing users’ attention. This chart visually represents the purse values of various 2024 tournaments, with each bubble’s size correlating directly to the tournament’s purse amount. This scaling approach allows users to quickly grasp the relative significance of each tournament’s prize, enhancing comparative analysis at a glance.

To further improve user interactivity, I incorporated a tooltip feature that activates on hover. When users hover over a bubble, it subtly enlarges, providing a visual cue of selection. Simultaneously, a tooltip appears, displaying the tournament's name and purse value. This design choice not only aids in precise data reading but also adds an intuitive, responsive layer to the chart, making the data exploration both informative and visually dynamic.

VISUALISATION 3 PAGE

The third visualization is a dynamic radial chart designed to showcase the winner’s share for each golf tournament in 2024. In this chart, each tournament’s prize is represented by a dot, with larger prize amounts placed further from the center, making them more visually prominent. The dot size is also scaled to reflect the prize amount, allowing users to instantly gauge which tournaments offer the biggest rewards. Smaller prizes are positioned closer to the center, creating a layered and easy-to-read visual hierarchy.

To enhance user interactivity, the radial chart includes a slider function that smoothly transitions the dots outward, allowing users to compare the winner’s shares side by side. Additionally, three checkboxes enable users to filter dots by the number of FedEx Cup points awarded in each tournament, making it easy to focus on events of specific competitive importance. As users toggle these options, the dots move around the chart, creating a visually engaging experience that invites further exploration and comparison. This interactive design adds depth to the data, making it both informative and enjoyable to use.

STYLE GUIDE

COLOUR PALETTE

I put a lot of thought into the color palette, opting for a blend of neutral tones with strategic pops of color. The neutral shades create a calming, inviting atmosphere, which was important for the overall aesthetic, while the brighter accents help guide users’ attention to key interactive elements like buttons and links. I wanted the palette to be visually appealing but also functional, ensuring that users are naturally drawn to important parts of the site without feeling overwhelmed by too many loud colors.

FONTS

For the website, I chose a combination of modern, easy-to-read fonts that would enhance both readability and accessibility. I wanted the headings to stand out, so I selected a bold, stylish typeface that grabs attention, while keeping the body text more straightforward and legible for longer reading sessions. It was important to me that the fonts complement each other, creating a sense of professionalism and unity across the site. I also made sure the fonts would display well on all devices, maintaining consistency regardless of screen size.

INSPIRATIONS

ST. ANDREWS LINKS

St. Andrews Links Website

The St Andrews Links website is a comprehensive guide to everything related to the historic golf courses in St Andrews, Scotland. It features detailed information about each course, booking options, and available services, such as equipment hire and caddy services. The site also highlights the history and significance of St Andrews as the "Home of Golf." What stood out to me was how the website beautifully balances functionality and user experience, with clear navigation and sections dedicated to golf events, sustainability efforts, and even a shop for official merchandise. The layout makes it easy to find exactly what you need, whether it’s information on a specific course or help with planning your visit.

When designing my own website, I drew inspiration from the St Andrews Links site, particularly in its use of clear navigation and structured content. I wanted my website to reflect a similar ease of access, where visitors can quickly find the information they’re looking for without being overwhelmed. The way St Andrews Links incorporates multimedia elements—like images and videos—while maintaining a professional yet welcoming tone, also influenced how I designed visual elements for my site. The overall user experience was a benchmark for creating a smooth, engaging journey for my own website's visitors.

RALPH LAUREN

Ralph Lauren Website

The Ralph Lauren website is a perfect example of elegance and high-end fashion combined with a user-friendly shopping experience. The sleek design, clean layouts, and use of luxurious imagery instantly communicate the brand's identity. I noticed how they use neutral colors like white, beige, and black to maintain a sophisticated look, while the large, high-quality photos of their products emphasize detail and craftsmanship. The navigation is intuitive, making it easy to browse between categories like men's, women's, and children's apparel, as well as their home decor section. Their storytelling element, "RL Stories," adds another layer by offering visitors a more immersive experience into the lifestyle associated with the brand.

Using Ralph Lauren’s website as inspiration, I aimed to apply similar design choices to my own project. I wanted to create a clean and polished look by adopting their use of simple, neutral backgrounds and well-placed images to give my website an elegant and organized feel. I also took note of how they highlight products with high-resolution visuals and made sure to focus on quality images that are central to my site. Additionally, I liked how they subtly integrated storytelling to build a stronger connection with visitors, and I drew from this idea to enhance the narrative element of my site. The balance of luxury and functionality they achieve served as a great model for my design choices.

UI ELEMENTS

DESIGN CHOICES

When it came to the overall design, I prioritized simplicity and a user-centered approach. I aimed for a layout that was clean and organized, allowing content to be easily digestible without overwhelming users. I utilized whitespace and consistent alignment to make the pages feel balanced, while the strategic use of color and typography helped guide users through the site. Every design decision was made with the user in mind, ensuring that the website was visually appealing, but most importantly, functional and easy to use.


For the navbar, my goal was to make navigation as seamless and intuitive as possible. I kept the design clean and simple, avoiding any unnecessary clutter. I also prioritized responsiveness, so the navbar adjusts nicely on different devices, from desktops to mobile phones. I felt that a well-organized, minimalistic navbar would help users stay focused on the content rather than get distracted by the navigation itself.


Images play an essential role on the website, so I chose them carefully to support and complement the content. I made sure each image has a purpose, avoiding the temptation to overload the site with visuals. Optimizing the images for quick load times without sacrificing quality was also a priority, as I didn’t want users to experience slow performance. I paid particular attention to providing alt-text for each image, ensuring the site is accessible for everyone, including those using screen readers. It was crucial for me to strike a balance between aesthetics and functionality with the images.

ACCESSIBILITY

Accessibility is something I focused on from the beginning. I wanted the site to be usable for everyone, so I incorporated features like alt-text for images, sufficient color contrast, and a layout that is both clean and easy to navigate. I made sure the fonts were scalable and responsive so users could adjust the text size to suit their needs. I also tested the site to ensure it could be navigated entirely via keyboard, catering to users with mobility impairments. My aim was to create an inclusive design that doesn’t compromise on appearance or usability.

CONTRAST CHECKER

One of the tools I relied on during the design process was a contrast checker. It was important to me that the site met web accessibility standards, so I used the checker to ensure there was enough contrast between text and background colors. This way, users with visual impairments or those browsing in different lighting conditions would have no trouble reading the content. I feel this step added another layer of inclusivity to the design, ensuring that the website is as user-friendly as possible.
